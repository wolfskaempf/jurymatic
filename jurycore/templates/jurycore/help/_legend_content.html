Legend for the jurymatic evaluation table

Language Skills (LS)

Positive

  • expresses their thoughts in an understandable way
  • uses appropriate language and the right terms
  • understands what fellow delegates say
  • uses also new vocabulary that has been learnt during Committee Work
  • helps others out, when they have language problems

Negative

  • uses too colloquial language or swear words
  • cannot follow the discussion
  • switches to their native language
  • does not try to circumscribe unknown terms (but switches to their native language)

Understanding of the Topic (UT)

Positive

  • knows facts
  • can explain terms to others
  • brings up new points
  • takes into account recent developments within the respective topic
  • presents arguments in a coherent way
  • has a European view on the topic

Negative

  • discusses the topic only from a regional or national perspective and fails to include the European dimension
  • presents wrong facts
  • only recites general statements
  • cannot name the source of their information

Teamwork (TW)

Positive

  • makes compromises
  • distributes tasks fairly amongst committee members
  • asks calm delegates for their opinion
  • takes into account and reacts to others' arguments
  • looks at the person speaking
  • tries to resolve tensions
  • summarises parts of the discussion for others

Negative

  • interrupts others
  • leads separate discussion with neighbours instead of following the group discussion
  • does not try to understand somebody else's perspective
  • does not take part in the discussion actively

Target Orientation (TO)

Positive

  • moderates the Committee so that a compromise is found
  • keeps time limit and schedule in mind
  • notes points down
  • realises when everyone agrees on a certain point
  • summarises a discussion
  • has a vision or goal for the topic

Negative

  • comes back to points that were discussed before
  • fosters voting
  • loses the overview
  • sticks to details

Overall Impression (OI)

  • The above mentioned criteria cannot cover all skills a delegate has. Therefore, the overall impression is not the mean average of the above mentioned criteria, but the juror's overall impression of a delegate. It can be different from the criteria above and include other criteria that might be important.
